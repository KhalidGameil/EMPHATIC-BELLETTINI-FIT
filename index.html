Summary of Analysis:

  • Analysis was performed on 30GeV/c graphite target data (Runs 250-251)
  • Fitting the Coulomb and Elastic components of the t distribution:

    • \[\begin{equation}\begin{split} \frac{d\sigma}{dt} &= \frac{\pi}{p^2} \left(C^2 + I^2\right)\\ &= \pi \left(\frac{2 Z r_e m c}{t}\right)^2 \times \exp{-\frac{a^2}{3\hbar^2} |t|} + \frac{1}{\pi} \left( \frac{\sigma_{tot}}{4\hbar} \right)^2 \times \exp{-B|t|}. \\ \end{split} \end{equation}\]

  • Bellettini subtracts the coulomb component because there are no fitted values

    • $a = 2.42E-15meter$

    m = mass of electron

    $r_e$ = radius of electron

  • Fitting the elastic region seperately:

    • \[\begin{equation} \label{eq:quasi2} \begin{split} \frac{d\sigma}{dt} &\approx N(A) (92.77) \exp{-10.2|t|}. \end{split} \end{equation}\]

Previous Issues with $\chi^2$ (Monicelli):

  • The $\chi^2$ is very sensitive to the lower bound of elastic/Coulomb fit:
    • Not fitting at $t \approx 0$ because the Coulomb equation diverges at that point
    • Due to the real part of the elastic scatter being neglected the cross term be-tween the real component and the Coulomb scatter is not considered
    • The Coulomb region also contains an imaginary phase that also needs to be considered for the cross term
    • Bellettini's minimum t-value for the elastic/Coulomb region is $\approx 0.002 GeV^2c^{-2}$
